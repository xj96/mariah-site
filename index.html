Subjects Offered

Pre-K & Kindergarten (Ages 3 - 5):
  1. Counting and recognizing numbers (1-20 and beyond).
  2. Basic shapes (circle, square, triangle, rectangle).
  3. Patterns (simple patterns like ABAB).
  4. Introduction to addition and subtraction (very basic).
Grade 1 - 2:
  1. Addition and subtraction (up to 20 or higher).
  2. Place value (understanding tens and ones).
  3. Introduction to multiplication and division (basics).
  4. Time (telling time to the hour and half-hour).
  5. Money (identifying coins and simple transactions).
  6. Measurement (length, weight, capacity).
  7. Basic geometry (2D and 3D shapes, symmetry).
Grade 3 - 5:
  1. Multiplication and division (including multi-digit numbers).
  2. Fractions (understanding, adding, subtracting, multiplying, and dividing).
  3. Decimals (introduction and basic operations).
  4. Measurement (converting units, area, and perimeter).
  5. Time (elapsed time, digital and analog clocks).
  6. Geometry (angles, polygons, congruence, symmetry).
  7. Data and statistics (collecting and interpreting data, graphs).
  8. Introduction to basic algebraic concepts (variables, equations).
Grade 6 - 8:
  1. Advanced arithmetic (ratios, proportions, percentages).
  2. Exponents and square roots.
  3. Integers, rational numbers, and real numbers.
  4. Algebra (solving equations and inequalities, graphing functions).
  5. Geometry (advanced topics like volume, surface area, similar and congruent figures).
  6. Statistics and probability (data analysis, probability concepts).
  7. Introduction to basic concepts of trigonometry.
  8. Pre-algebra and introductory algebra concepts.
  9. Linear equations and systems of equations.

  3. Vocabulary development.
  4. Basic sight words.
  5. Simple sentence structure (subject-verb-object).

Grade 1 - 2:

Grade 3 - 5:
  1. Advanced phonics and word analysis.
  2. Reading comprehension (identifying main ideas, details, and making inferences).
  3. Vocabulary development and context clues.
  4. Writing paragraphs and essays (introduction, body, conclusion).
  5. Spelling and grammar (parts of speech, subject-verb agreement).
  6. Research skills (using references and citing sources).
  7. Creative writing (narrative, descriptive, and persuasive).

Grade 6 - 8:

  7. Research skills (conducting research, citing sources).
  8. Creative writing (short stories, poetry, essays).
  9. Public speaking and presentation skills.
  10. Editing and revising written work for clarity and coherence.

Math Test Prep:
  1. Problem Solving: Teaching students how to approach and solve math problems effectively, including word problems.
  2. Math Concepts: Reviewing and reinforcing fundamental math concepts, including addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, fractions, decimals, percentages, and geometry.
  3. Algebra Basics: Introducing basic algebraic concepts, such as solving equations and working with variables (more common in middle school test prep).
  4. Data Analysis: Covering data interpretation, graphs, charts, and statistics, including concepts like mean, median, and mode.
  5. Geometry: Reviewing geometry concepts, such as area, perimeter, volume, and properties of geometric shapes.
  6. Measurement: Teaching measurement concepts, including conversions between units and estimation.
  7. Math Vocabulary: Building a strong math vocabulary to understand and solve problems.
Reading & Writing Test Prep
  1. Reading Comprehension: Focusing on strategies for understanding and analyzing reading passages, including identifying main ideas, supporting details, and making inferences.
  2. Vocabulary Building: Expanding vocabulary through reading and word study to understand context and improve reading comprehension.
  3. Critical Reading: Developing critical reading skills to evaluate arguments and identify author's purpose and tone.
  4. Writing Skills: Teaching the writing process, including brainstorming, outlining, drafting, revising, and editing. Emphasizing clarity, organization, and grammar.
  5. Essay Writing: Preparing students to write essays, which may include persuasive essays, literary analysis essays, and informative essays.
  6. Grammar and Mechanics: Reviewing and reinforcing grammar rules, punctuation, and sentence structure.
  7. Spelling and Vocabulary: Improving spelling and vocabulary skills to enhance writing and reading comprehension.
  8. Test-Taking Strategies: Providing strategies for managing time, eliminating answer choices, and approaching different types of questions (multiple-choice, short answer, essay).
  9. Practice Tests: Administering practice tests similar to the format of the actual standardized tests to familiarize students with the test structure and pacing.
  10. Reading Fluency: For younger students, developing reading fluency by practicing reading aloud and building confidence in their reading abilities.
